CSO Regulatory Matrix Typology:

1. Formation – Rules and procedures governing the creation, registration, suspension, or dissolution of civil society organizations (CSOs). Examples include registration requirements, government approval to operate, and penalties for unregistered activity.

2. Governance – Provisions that dictate how CSOs are structured internally. This includes leadership selection, board composition, membership regulations, and internal decision-making processes.

3. Operations – Laws that affect how CSOs function day-to-day. These include rules about holding meetings, protests, or assemblies; public communication; advocacy; and reporting non-financial activities.

4. Resources – Regulations on how CSOs acquire, manage, and report on resources. Covers domestic and foreign funding, taxation, reporting obligations, asset ownership, and limitations on fundraising.
